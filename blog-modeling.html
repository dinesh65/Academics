Why Mathematical Modeling Matters

Mathematics becomes truly powerful when it is used to understand real-world problems. This is where mathematical modeling enters.

Mathematical modeling is the process of translating real-life problems into mathematical language, solving the mathematical problem, and then interpreting the solution back in the real-world context.

In ecology, epidemiology, economics, and social sciences, models help us understand growth, decay, competition, cooperation, and stability of systems.

Students should learn mathematics not only as formulas but as a language to describe the world.
