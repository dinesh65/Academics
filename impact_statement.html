Research identity
I study how network topology governs the stability, persistence, and partitionability of dynamical systems — with applications ranging from spatial ecology and metapopulation management to general network science and graph algorithms.

The central object in my research is the graph Laplacian — its spectrum, particularly the Fiedler value (algebraic connectivity), encodes surprisingly precise information about whether a networked system will persist, collapse, or admit a meaningful partition. My work translates this algebraic structure into actionable results: conditions under which ecological metapopulations survive habitat fragmentation, algorithms for fragmenting networks while preserving stability in each piece, and stability criteria for reaction-diffusion processes on heterogeneous graphs.

Research pillars
Networked dynamical systems

Stability and persistence of ecological and biological systems modelled on graphs; discrete reaction-diffusion dynamics; role of the Fiedler value in governing collective behaviour.

Graph partitioning with dynamics

Sustainable ecological partitioning of metapopulation networks; extension of NP-hard static graph partitioning to systems with node-level dynamics; computer algorithms verified on ecological benchmarks.

Teaching philosophy & practice

My pedagogy is built on a simple conviction: formal definitions should arrive only after students feel the need for them. I call this necessity-first (or frustration-first) teaching. The frustration is the lesson.

Discovery problem sets — students encounter the inadequacy of existing tools before the new concept is named; the definition then lands as relief, not imposition.

Interdisciplinary projects — MAT 101 (Linear Algebra & DEs) assignments integrated with data structures and algorithmic thinking; mathematics taught as a live tool, not a finished museum piece.

Visualisation & experimentation — GeoGebra, Desmos, flipped classroom, weekly short assessments, WhatsApp discussion groups for in-course Q&A; tested across NIT Jalandhar, BML Munjal, and NIIT University.

Mathematics Club — founded and ran a student outreach club at NIIT University focused on mathematical popularisation, problem culture, and science communication.
